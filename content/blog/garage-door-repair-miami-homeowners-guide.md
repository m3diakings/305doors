## Why Garage Doors Fail More Often in Miami

Before getting into specific problems, it's important to understand why garage doors in Miami tend to fail more often than in other places.

### 1. Humidity and Salt Air

Moisture in the air can lead to:

- Rust on springs and cables  
- Corrosion on metal parts  
- Wear on moving components  

### 2. Daily Use

Garage doors are often the main entry point to the home, which means:

- Multiple cycles per day  
- Faster wear and tear  

### 3. Sudden Weather Changes

Storms and heat can affect:

- Electrical components  
- Door alignment  
- Sensor performance  

## Most Common Garage Door Problems (And What They Mean)

Let's go through the issues we see most often.

### 1. Garage Door Won't Open or Close

This is the most common problem.

**Possible causes:**

- Dead remote batteries  
- Misaligned sensors  
- Opener malfunction  
- Power issues  

**What you can check:**

- Replace remote batteries  
- Make sure sensors are aligned and clean  
- Check if the opener has power  

If none of this works, it's time to look into professional repair.

### 2. Broken Springs

Garage door springs do most of the heavy lifting.

When they break, you might notice:

- A loud snapping sound  
- The door won't lift  
- The door feels extremely heavy  

**Warning:** This is not a DIY repair. Springs are under high tension and can be dangerous.

### 3. Garage Door Off Track

If your door looks uneven or gets stuck while moving, it may be off track.

**Causes:**

- Impact (car hitting the door)  
- Worn rollers  
- Loose hardware  

This requires proper realignment to avoid further damage.

### 4. Noisy Garage Door

A loud garage door is usually a warning sign.

**Common reasons:**

- Lack of lubrication  
- Worn rollers  
- Loose components  

Ignoring this can lead to bigger issues later.

### 5. Garage Door Opens Then Stops

If your door starts moving but stops midway, it could be:

- Sensor obstruction  
- Track issues  
- Opener settings  

## What You Can Safely Do Before Calling a Professional

There are a few basic things you can check safely:

**Check the power source**

Make sure the opener is plugged in and working.

**Inspect the sensors**

They should be aligned and free of dirt.

**Look for obstructions**

Check tracks for debris or objects.

**Test the remote**

Try using the wall button instead.

## What You Should NEVER Try to Fix Yourself

This is extremely important.

Avoid touching or repairing:

- Springs  
- Cables  
- Tension systems  
- Motor internals  

These components can cause serious injury if handled incorrectly.

## When It's Time to Call a Professional
